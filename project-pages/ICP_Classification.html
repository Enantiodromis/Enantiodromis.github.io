Breaking Ground: Pioneering Non-Invasive Intracranial Pressure Assessment through PPG Signal Classification
This study is the first of its kind to explore the feasibility of utilising photoplethysmography signals to effectively classify normotensive and hypertensive intracranial pressure in patients with traumatic brain injury. (In review)
Project Details / Background
A dataset was constructed, comprising of synchronized data from a non-invasive optical sensor and an invasive gold standard intracranial pressure monitor from 31 patients. From this data, 202 features were extracted for each one-minute window of non- invasive data. A total of 25 classification models were evaluated.

The results demonstrate the potential of photoplethysmography data in accurately classifying normotensive and hy- pertensive intracranial pressure levels. The optimal model achieving an accuracy of 85.74%. The reported F1 scores further validate the studyâ€™s efficacy with 85.75% and 85.69% for nor- motensive and hypertensive classes respectively. This study establishes a credible foundation for further research in this field.

These initial findings pave the way for the development and testing of regression models for intracranial pressure assessment ultimately leading towards the aim of developing continuous, non-invasive intracranial pressure monitoring techniques.
